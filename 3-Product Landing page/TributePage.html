Muhammad Ali Jinnah

Lawyer | Politician | Founder of Pakistan

The Life Journey/Timeline of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

  • 1876 - Born in Wazir Mansion, Karachi
  • 1880 - Joined Gokal Das Tej Primary School in Bombay, later Cathedral and John Connon School
  • 1885 - Sindh-Madrasa-tul-Islam
  • 1888 - Matriculation from Bombay University
  • 1892 - 1893 Married to 'Emibai Jinnah' at the age of 16
  • 1892 - Left Karachi for a degree in law from 'Lincoln's Inn' London
  • 1895 - At the age of 19 was 'called to the bar' and then moved to Karachi for a short time
  • 1896 - At the age of 20, Jinnah began his practice in Bombay, the only Muslim barrister in the city
  • 1904 - Jinnah began political life by attending the Congress's twentieth annual meeting, in Bombay
  • 1909 - Gain his first elective office as Bombay's Muslim representative on the 'Imperial Legislative Council'
  • 1913 - Jinnah became a member of the 'Muslim League'
  • 1916 - Jinnah became the president of the 'Muslim League'
  • 1918 – 1929 Married to 'Rattanbai Jinnah'
  • 1925 - Elected President of 'All India Postal Staff Union'
  • 1930 - 1932 Round Table Conferences were held in London, to discuss the political deadlock and constitutional settlement of British India
  • 1934 - Transformation of the Muslim League into a mass party representative of all sections of the Muslim community
  • 1938 - Jinnah addresses the Muslim League session at Patna
  • 1947 - Jinnah became the first Governor-General of Pakistan, his sister Fatimah Jinnah became the "Mother of the Nation". He also became the president of Pakistan's constituent assembly
  • 1948 - Jinnah died due to illness and has been buried in ‎Mazar-e-Quaid, Karachi
